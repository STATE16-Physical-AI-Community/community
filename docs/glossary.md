# Glossary

## Physical AI
AI systems that interact with, reason about, or generate actions for the physical world. Examples include robotics, drones, autonomous systems, embodied AI, VLA models, and world models.

## Physical AI Assurance
The practice of evaluating whether AI-generated states, actions, trajectories, or decisions are safe, admissible, and consistent with real-world constraints.

## Action Admissibility
A test of whether an AI-generated action is allowed under physical, operational, safety, and policy constraints.

## State Consistency
A test of whether the estimated or generated state of the system is consistent with observations, goals, constraints, and physical reality.

## Runtime Assurance
A layer that evaluates system behavior during operation, before or while actions are executed.

## Physical AI Hallucination

A situation where an AI system generates a state, action, trajectory, or decision that appears plausible but violates physical, operational, or safety constraints.

## World Model Validity

A question of whether the internal representations learned by a world model correspond to states that can actually exist in the real world.
