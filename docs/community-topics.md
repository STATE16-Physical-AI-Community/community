# Community Topics
This community explores broad topics related to the reliability, safety, and evaluation of AI systems that interact with or reason about the physical world.
The goal is to create an open professional space for discussion, learning, paper reviews, technical examples, and shared terminology around Physical AI Assurance.

## Core theme
How can AI systems that operate in or reason about the physical world be evaluated more responsibly?

## Discussion areas
1. Reliability challenges in Physical AI systems
2. Safety considerations for AI-generated actions and plans
3. Failure modes in robotics, drones, and autonomous systems
4. Differences between digital AI mistakes and physical-world AI mistakes
5. The role of constraints in evaluating AI behavior
6. How simulation, benchmarks, and toy examples can support safer AI development
7. How researchers and engineers can document physical AI failures
8. How open-source tools can support education and experimentation in Physical AI
9. How the community can review and discuss relevant academic papers
10. How to build a shared vocabulary for Physical AI Assurance

## Example discussion prompts
- What kinds of mistakes can AI systems make when operating in the physical world?
- Why is task success not always enough when evaluating physical systems?
- What are useful examples of unsafe or invalid AI-generated behavior?
- Which papers, datasets, or open-source tools are relevant to this field?
- What simple toy examples can help explain Physical AI Assurance to new contributors?

## Scope
This document is intended to guide open community discussion.
